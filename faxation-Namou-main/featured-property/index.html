Can foreigners buy freehold land in Ras Al Khaimah?

Yes. Since 2005, RAK has designated several freehold zones — including Al Marjan Island, Al Hamra, and Mina Al Arab — where foreign nationals can acquire 100% ownership of land and property. No local sponsor or UAE residency is required.

Reference: Khaleej Times — UAE Property Coverage

Can I buy RAK land remotely without visiting the UAE?

Yes. Foreign buyers can complete a full RAK land transaction remotely with a signed Power of Attorney to a licensed UAE representative. Due diligence, contract signing, escrow, and title transfer can all be arranged digitally — a visit to the UAE is optional, not required.

Reference: Khaleej Times — Remote Property Purchase UAE

What ROI can I realistically expect from RAK land?

Returns vary by strategy. Land appreciation in prime freehold zones has tracked strong double-digit growth since 2022. Development plays (build-to-sell) typically target 20–30% margins, while hospitality joint-ventures yield operating returns plus exit appreciation. Projections always depend on deal structure and timing.

Reference: Bayut — RAK Property Market Reports

What fees are involved in a RAK property transaction?

Expect approximately 4–5% in total transaction costs: RAK Municipality transfer fee (typically 2%), real estate agency commission (2%), title deed issuance, and legal/POA costs. Fees are paid at transfer and are typically split per the sale agreement.

Reference: Bayut — Cost of Buying Property in the UAE
