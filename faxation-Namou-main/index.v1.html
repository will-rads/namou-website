Can foreigners buy freehold land in Ras Al Khaimah?

Yes. Since 2005, Ras Al Khaimah has designated several freehold zones — including Al Marjan Island, Al Hamra, and Mina Al Arab — where foreign nationals can acquire 100% ownership of land and property. No local sponsor or UAE residency is required.

Reference: Khaleej Times — UAE Property Coverage

Can I get a Golden Visa by investing in RAK real estate?

Yes. A property investment of AED 2 million or more in any UAE emirate, including RAK, qualifies you for a 10-year renewable Golden Visa. The property must be fully paid (or mortgaged with a significant deposit) and can include land or completed real estate.

Reference: UAE Government Portal — Golden Visa Eligibility

What taxes apply to rental income and capital gains in RAK?

The UAE levies no personal income tax, no capital gains tax, and no tax on rental income for individuals. Corporate tax of 9% applies only to businesses with profits above AED 375,000. VAT of 5% applies to commercial property but not to residential land transactions.

Reference: PwC — UAE Tax Summary

Why invest in RAK instead of Dubai?

RAK offers entry prices significantly below comparable Dubai markets, higher gross rental yields, and a fast-growing pipeline anchored by the Wynn Al Marjan Resort. With freehold ownership, zero income tax, and 45 minutes from Dubai, RAK is positioned as the UAE's next growth corridor.

Reference: Knight Frank — Wealth Report & UAE Market Outlook

How is the Wynn Resort affecting property values in Al Marjan?

Wynn Al Marjan — the first Wynn outside the US and the region's first integrated resort — is projected to drive significant appreciation across Al Marjan Island and the surrounding area. Hospitality-zoned land near the resort has seen demand accelerate sharply since construction began.

Reference: Arabian Business — Wynn RAK Coverage
